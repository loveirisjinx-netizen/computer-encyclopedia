Computer History

The history of computers dates back to ancient times with mechanical counting devices. Modern computers have evolved through several generations, from vacuum tubes to transistors, integrated circuits, and microprocessors, becoming faster, smaller, and more powerful.

Early Computers

Early computing machines like the abacus, mechanical calculators, and punch-card machines laid the foundation for modern computers.

Networking

Computer networking involves connecting multiple devices to share resources, data, and communication. Networks can be small (LAN) or large (WAN, internet).

Key Components

  • Router: Directs data traffic between networks.
  • Switch: Connects multiple devices in a LAN.
  • Ethernet Cable: Wired medium for data transfer.
  • Servers: Provide centralized resources to users.
  • Data Centers: Store large amounts of data and host services.
